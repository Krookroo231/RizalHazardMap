Rizal Disaster History

August 2, 1999:

Massive landslide in Cherry Hills subdivision, Antipolo, Rizal.
Caused by heavy rains from Typhoon Ising (Olga) and southwest monsoon.
Over 300 homes destroyed, 59 people killed.
Record-breaking 500 millimeters of rain fell from Aug. 1 to Aug. 3.
Landslide occurred around 7:00 p.m. on August 3, 1999.

July 24, 2021:

1,700 individuals evacuated in three municipalities in Rizal due to heavy monsoon rains.
Taytay: 29 families (121 individuals) in three evacuation centers.
San Mateo: 45 families (216 individuals) in one evacuation center.
Rodriguez: 365 families (1,439 individuals) in six evacuation centers.
More than 300 individuals evacuated in Cainta due to flooding.

August 5, 2022:

Heavy rainfall in Rizal due to LPA and Southwest Monsoon.
5 incidents monitored: 4 flooding, 1 collapsed structure.
51 families (214 persons) affected.
50 families (210 persons) took shelter in 5 evacuation centers.
1 family (4 persons) stayed with relatives/friends.
Municipality of Cardona experienced power interruption.
1 damaged riprap in Binangonan.

Rodriguez Rizal Earthquake


E. Rodriguez Avenue in Rizal, Philippines, is located within a seismically active region. The entire Philippines is situated along the Pacific Ring of Fire. Therefore, like many other areas in the country, E. Rodriguez Avenue in Rizal is also considered to be at risk of earthquakes.

To prepare for earthquakes:
1. Create and practice an emergency plan.
2. Secure heavy furniture and objects.
3. Reinforce buildings and structures.
4. Conduct regular inspections of buildings.
5. Educate everyone on proper earthquake safety procedures.
6. Store emergency supplies.

Rodriguez Rizal Flood


Low-lying areas near the Marikina River, San Mateo River, and their tributaries are prone to flooding, particularly during heavy rainfall and typhoon events.

To prevent floods:

1. Maintain and clear drainage systems.
2. Construct flood barriers or levees.
3. Implement land-use planning and zoning regulations.
4. Preserve natural water retention areas like wetlands.
5. Develop effective flood early warning systems.
6. Promote responsible waste management to prevent blockage of waterways.
